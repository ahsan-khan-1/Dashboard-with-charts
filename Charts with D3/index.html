Group 11 Project 2


Chart1: The proportion of the top 100 YouTube channels in each category.

We decided to represent the proportion of the top 100 YouTube channels in each category with a pie chart because it effectively showcases the relative sizes of different categories as parts of a whole. Pie charts provide a clear visual representation of how each category compares to others in terms of its share, making it easy for viewers to grasp the distribution at a glance.

Chart2: Relationship between the ‘likes’ and ‘subscribers’

A scatter plot chart effectively visualizes the correlation between 'likes' and 'subscribers,' displaying the distribution of data points clearly. It helps identify patterns, trends, or outliers, making it easier to understand how the number of likes varies with the number of subscribers across different YouTube channels.

Chart 3: Each country's number of Youtubers

We decided to represent the number of YouTubers in each country with a bar graph because it effectively compares the quantity of YouTubers across different countries. Bar graphs allow for easy visualization of the data, highlighting differences in the number of creators by country. The vertical bar provides a clear, straightforward way to compare the values, making it simple to identify which countries have the most or least YouTubers.

Chart 4: The top 5 YouTube channels' average view changes every year

Chart 4 uses a line chart to represent the average yearly view changes for the top 5 YouTube channels, effectively showing trends over time. Line charts are ideal for visualizing how values change across a continuous range, such as years, making it easier to track increases, decreases, or consistent patterns in viewership. This format provides a clear representation of the channels' performance dynamics over multiple years.

Chart 5: The top 5 YouTube channels’ Quarterly income.

We decided to represent the top 5 YouTube channels’ quarterly income with a grouped bar chart because it allows for an easy comparison of income across multiple categories (quarters) for each channel. Grouped bar charts display data in distinct clusters, making it straightforward to observe differences in income trends among the channels and across different quarters within the same visualization. This format provides a clear and organized way to analyze variations in earnings over time.

Chart6: The number of YouTube channels by category

Chart 6 displays the number of YouTube channels by category interactively based on the selected country, with 'IN' (India) as the default. The chart title updates dynamically to reflect the chosen country, ensuring clarity. For mobile users, a dropdown menu or autocomplete search box provides a compact and user-friendly way to input the country.

Chart7: The category that has most followers

A donut chart effectively highlights the category with the most followers by showcasing proportions in a visually appealing way. It allows for easy comparison between categories while emphasizing the dominant one.

Chart8: The country that has the most youtubers in the top 100 list.

Chart 8 uses a pie chart to represent the country with the most YouTubers in the top 100 list, making it easy to compare the proportions of YouTubers from different countries. The circular layout highlights the dominant country while providing a clear visual breakdown of the distribution among others.

Chart9: The name of the channel with the most subscribers

A horizontal bar graph with a scrollable view is ideal for displaying data when there are many categories or items to compare. This format allows for easy readability of category names, especially when they are lengthy, and provides a clear visual representation of the data. The scrollable feature ensures that all categories can be included without overcrowding the chart, making it user-friendly and space-efficient.
